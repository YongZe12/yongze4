Definition

SDG 15: Life on Land aims to protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, manage forests sustainably, halt and reverse land degradation, and halt biodiversity loss.

It focuses on ensuring the conservation of plants, animals, and their habitats so that life can thrive on land.

Causes of Land Degradation & Biodiversity Loss

  • Deforestation for farming and urban expansion
  • Pollution from factories and human waste
  • Poor land management and overgrazing
  • Climate change affecting habitats
  • Illegal hunting and poaching of animals

Effects on Nature

  • Loss of animal and plant species (extinction)
  • Soil erosion and poor crop growth
  • Floods, droughts, and climate instability
  • Less clean air and water
  • Unbalanced food chains

Solutions for Life on Land

  • Plant more trees and protect forests
  • Recycle and reduce pollution
  • Support sustainable farming
  • Learn about and respect wildlife
  • Stop hunting endangered animals
  • Participate in local clean-up activities!
